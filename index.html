Why is this converter needed? Detailed context

            
            

                
                

                    
                    

                        

                            

                                The Kodak Charmera is an vintage-style camera that outputs video in a legacy .AVI container. Specifically, it encodes video frames as individual JPEGs (M-JPEG codec) and audio tracks in raw PCM format.
                            

                            

                                Modern smartphones (such as Apple iPhones, Samsung Galaxy S23/S24, or Google Pixel devices) have removed support for legacy AVI wrappers and raw M-JPEG formats to preserve hardware-decoding efficiency. 
                            

                            

                                This app converts the video to H.264 (AVC) video and AAC audio inside a native .MP4 file, which can be played instantly in any modern device photo library, messaging app, or browser.
                            

                        

                    

                


                
                

                    
                    

                        

                            

                                Many online converters force compressed aspect ratios, re-scale resolution, or degrade colors. This tool is configured specifically for the Charmera output specifications:
                            

                            
    
                                
  • Resolution: Locked to native 1440x1080 (HD 4:3).
    • 
                                
  • Framerate: Native 30 frames per second.
    • 
                                
  • Video Quality: CRF 18 parameter encodes H.264 in a visually lossless translation directly from the original M-JPEG streams.
    • 
                                
  • Audio Quality: AAC compression at 128kbps, preserving perfect stereo audio sync.
    •

You can even disconnect your internet entirely after opening the app, and it will still transcode your files!
